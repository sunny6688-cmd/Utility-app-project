What is SHA-256?

The above SHA-256 generator allows you to easily compute hashes and checksums, but what are they exactly?
SHA256 is a part of the SHA-2 (Secure Hash Algorithm 2) family of one-way cryptographic functions, developed in 2001 by the United States National Security Agency (NSA).
Hashing functions are cryptographic devices that take as input any string of characters or a file of any type, and then output a computed collision-resistant hash.
Collision resistance means that nobody should be able to find two different inputs that produce the same hash.
